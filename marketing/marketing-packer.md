# Marketing Packer

## Positioning
- Product: `mupla-front`
- Brand / owner: `fluone`
- Positioning angle: professional frontend studio delivery — avoid “AI-powered” unless a verifiable differentiator exists.

## Buyer Logic
- Buyer default: B2B SaaS
- Primary pain: inconsistent frontend quality, handoff friction, slow delivery
- Primary outcome: predictable studio-grade UI at startup speed
- Call-to-action angle: try the workflow, not the buzzword

## Channel Rules
- Lead with evidence, not hype.
- Use proof: performance numbers, structured workflow, repeatable delivery.
- If social proof is unavailable, use transparent process and specs instead.

## Signup Goal
- Convert interest into signup with next-step clarity
- Copy should imply a working system, not a product concept

## Content Constraints
- No fluff, vague platitudes, or generic AI language.
- Prefer exact words: “delivery workflow,” “component baseline,” “pipeline,” “frontend studio.”
- If brand keywords are missing locally, assume nothing speculative.
