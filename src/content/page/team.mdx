      ## Our story

      mupla began in a living room. A group of families wanted their children to grow up knowing their neighbors — and they wanted to be the kind of community that shows up when someone is in need. Over the years, we've grown into a year-round foundation serving more than 1,200 neighbors each year, supported by 48 weekly volunteers and a small, committed staff.

      We are still that same group of families, just with more chairs at the table. Most of our team is volunteer-led, and we keep our staff small so that the bulk of every dollar reaches the people it serves.

      ## How the foundation is governed

      mupla is a registered nonprofit with a small board of community volunteers who provide governance, fiduciary oversight, and strategic direction. Board members serve two-year terms and are nominated by current members at our annual meeting. The board meets quarterly and publishes minutes to our members on request.

      Our day-to-day operations are run by a small staff team (above) supported by 48 weekly volunteers across our six core programs. The Zakat committee operates independently of staff and reports directly to the board.

      ## Conflict of interest

      mupla maintains a written conflict-of-interest policy. All staff and board members disclose annually any relationships with vendors, partners, or recipients that could present a financial conflict. Zakat committee members recuse themselves from any request involving someone with whom they have a personal or business relationship.
