      ## What we believe

      We believe faith is best lived in the company of others. The Qur'an calls us to enjoin the good and forbid the wrong,  and the scholars have always understood that this work is communal, not solitary. mupla exists to make that work easier, more organized, and more sustainable.

      We are guided by three principles:

      - **Rooted in tradition.** Our work draws from classical Islamic ethics of community, knowledge, and care. We don't invent new obligations; we try to live the ones we already have.
      - **Open to all.** You don't have to look or pray a certain way to be welcome at our iftars, our classes, or our volunteer days. We serve the whole community, and the whole community serves alongside us.
      - **Accountable to our neighbors.** Every program we run is shaped by the people it serves. We listen first, design second.

      ## A note on finances

      mupla is a registered nonprofit. We publish an annual report covering how every dollar is spent, who our major donors are, and what we accomplished. If you have questions about our finances, we want to hear them,  email us anytime.
